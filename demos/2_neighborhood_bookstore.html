Our Story

Patron Matilda Whitford envisioned a sanctuary where “learning might breathe freely.” The Athenaeum opened with 4,200 volumes, oil-lit lamps, and a promise of free access for all villagers. Over the following decades the collection swelled through bequests and adventurous acquisitions, from Babylonian clay tablets to first-print Kelmscott Press treasures.

  • Over 175,000 volumes in eight languages
  • Digitised manuscripts dating to the 14th century
  • Annual endowment supporting young researchers

1891 – Grand opening with lantern procession

1923 – Children’s Wing inaugurated beneath stained-glass skylight

1957 – Rare Prints & Map Room completed

2008 – Digital Media Hub launched with 30,000 e-resources

2024 – Winter Garden restored as community conservatory
